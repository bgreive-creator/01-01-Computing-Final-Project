    Well Being During Covid - Introduction

    Hello, this page displays how students in the United States and India were similarly and differently affected by the COVID-19 pandemic. Using a research pool of mostly college students, we analyzed how varying lifestyle habits such as sleep, screen time, physical activity, and social media use change a studentâ€™s overall well being. Comparing these metrics, this project points out patterns and highlights the key differences of the life of a college student between the United States and India throughout COVID.

    The U.S. data set contains over 10,000 individuals who were surveyed on their mental health and technology usage.The data was collected through self reported surveys that asked detailed questions. The India dataset was a collection of 1,182 students from schools in the Delhi National Capital Region. It was made available after being used for research in the Journal of Children and Youth Services Review.

    The Problem

    The results of online learning in the U.S. were pretty mixed with about 1/3 of students falling into each of positive, negative, and neutral categories. In India, The results were much less optimistic, with over half of the students reporting a negative experience of online school. Only 16% of students in India had a positive experience of online learning which was half that of the US. In both of these countries, students suffered greatly during the pandemic. The isolation and unsatisfaction with online learning lead to severe declines in adolescent well being.

    We will examine the causes of the students' well being and provide insights into how children might be able to live healthier lifestyles.
